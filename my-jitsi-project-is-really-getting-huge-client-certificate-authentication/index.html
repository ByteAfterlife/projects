My Jitsi Project is really getting huge (client certificate authentication)

Byte December 21, 2025

So in my last post of the Jitsi series, I explained how I did IP based blocking to control who can connect via a reverse proxy, but then I realized something. My IP can change, especially on mobile data. Which is bad, because then I wont be able to call on the go, very bad! So what did I do? Well I decided to try client certificate authentication, how it works is you basically install a certificate on your device (in this case, my phone) which is a file that basically proves your identity, then when my reverse proxy asks my phone to give a certificate and I pick the one, it checks if the certificate you provide was created by me and its valid, and if yes, then it grants you access, even if your IP isn't authorized, and if you dont provide a certificate, or dont have (a valid) one, but your IP is authorized, you're allowed access, and finally if your IP isn't authorized and you dont provide a valid certificate, you are just rejected access with my silly block page.
