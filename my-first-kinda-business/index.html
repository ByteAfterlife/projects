My first (kinda) business

Byte November 29, 2025

Back at this time, my parents were always running out of paper towels, I know, specific, but listen. I learned something, whenever they get paper towers, they drive 15 minutes to get some more, then I thought "What if, when we go shopping, I buy my own paper towels, store them somewhere in my house, then whenever my parents need them, instead of them driving 15 minutes, I sell them with a 'convenience fee' as a charge for saving them 15 minutes of driving" and it worked SOOO well! In 3 months I made $90, then we had to shut it down because we were moving. Of course, I love tech, so I made a LAN inventory tracker, integrated it with a keycloak server for SSO (I love keycloak) and it was a hit! It even sent out receipt emails to my parents via email, heres a screenshot of a email that got sent to my dad when he bought some paper towel once. I also decided to name it AnPlan because I thought it sounds good.

My parents liked this idea, especially my Dad, because I learned about how businesses work, they make or buy something, sell it at a higher price, and make profit. So I learned about what would be a fair profit percentage, and how to sell, manage inventory, and integrate with SSO, awesome project in my opinion!
