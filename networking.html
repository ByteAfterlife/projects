IP Basics

Firstly, lets start with IP's since they found alot of networking from what I know. So, there's two types of IP's. Theres "private IPs" and "public IPs" how they work is when you connect to a Wifi network or connect via ethernet, the router gives your device (phone, laptop, tablet, computer) this magic number called a Private IP, this is used for the router to identify you, and also so other devices can identify you, if your device is also hosting a server (mine has hosted many ;D) then other devices on that same network (example: wifi network) can use the private IP the router assigned you to access that server that your device is hosting. Now, what if you want people outside your (wifi) network to access your server? Well then people will need your Public IP and you'll need to change some router settings.

So, you're hosting a minecraft server on your computer and you want your friends to join but they arent on your wifi network? Cool! Here's an explination. First: Go to your router settings, now, this varies by router. Now, go to port forwarding (some call it something else), setup port forwarding (I can't be specific because again it's specific to your router brand and model, but you can usually just google "(your router creator and model) port forwarding tutorial" and you'll see step by step), then run the server on your computer, make sure your firewall allows the connections, and you're done. this applies to any server, not just minecraft. Now, your friends will use (your public IP):(port) to connect.

Also, just to reenforce private IP's are network specific, let me make 2 examples. First, technical one. Lets say my private IP the router gave my laptop is 192.168.56.12, well, if my friend on a different wifi network tries connecting to 192.168.56.12, they can't, the router will be confused, unless the router assigned 192.168.56.12 to another device like maybe his phone then he'd be connected to his phone, not my laptop. Here's a real life example, pretty easy to understand. Let's say you have a room, 1st door 2nd floor. If your friend went to his 2nd floor in his house and opened the 1st door, he wouldn't be in your room. Simple. 

Domains intro

But here's the problem, this is a good setup for a one time event, but what about for a 24/7 server? Well you may be wondering "What's the problem now? I set everything up, it should be ok forever now right?" WRONG. Ok so basically most router's that are for home use, like your or my parent's router change the public IP! I know, shameful. lol. So, how do we deal with that? Well so far my best solution is Domains. What are domains? Welcome to the next chapter.

Domains

Domains, you see them everywhere, google.com, example.com, this website right here is on a domain! But what are they? Ever wondered whats happening behind the scenes when you hit enter after typing "google.com" Probably not, I don't blame you honestly. WELL TOO BAD you've read everything before this part already so that means you're interested in what I have to say (thanks!) So, domains have these things called records, there are lots of types of records, to be honest I don't understand many, but most websites use CNAME or A, those basically point to a server from what I know. A points to an IP, I don't really know how CNAME works honestly. So, here's how you would setup a domain for your minecraft server.

Minecraft server sample

  1. Make sure you setup port forwarding, the minecraft server itself, and your firewall is ready
  2. Get your Public IP (you can search "what is my ip")
  3. Go to your domain and make an A record, set the value to the public IP you got
  4. You're done.

Lets say the domain you got is example.org, and your minecraft server is on port 25565, here's how it would work behind the scenes:

  1. Your friend types example.org:25565
  2. Your computer looks up the records for example.org
  3. It finds the A record leading to your public IP (lets say 1.1.1.1)
  4. It connects to 1.1.1.1:25565
  5. Your router (1.1.1.1) checks it's port forwarding settings for port 25565
  6. It see's you made a setting to forward port 25565 to port 25565 on your computer
  7. Your router connects to port 25565 on your computer and forwards all data sent through your friend's computer to yours.

So basically your router is this huge proxy that points connections to the right place.

The "I know your IP, I own you" theory

Ever been online and seen people threatening each other with their IP's or something, saying they know where the person lives or whatever, yeah thats actually a lie. Firstly, this IP they're talking about is the person's Public IP as we learned previously. Well, your ISP (the people that you pay for internet) has a building somewhere in your city or another city, and that's where your public IP's location is usually. Not your house. Sometimes, if your ISP doesn't have a building in your city, your public IP's location may even be set to the city beside yours because that's the closest building your ISP has.
